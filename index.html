Statement

Blake D. Lenoir is a contemporary surrealist multidisciplinary artist whose work moves across fine art, public space, and cultural and institutional platforms.

His practice spans original paintings, large-scale public murals, commissions, nonprofit work, brand partnerships, and youth mentorship with international reach. The largest scope of Lenoir's work operates inside Black Temporal Surrealism: a visual language where familiar imagery, cartoon distortion, and photorealistic portraiture collide to explore Black identity, emotional intelligence, mental health, and cultural memory through evoking intergenerational conversations.

His paintings are distortional entendres—images that hold more than one meaning at once, functioning as an inception point for conversations that matter.

Lenoir's intention reaches beyond resolution.
It is recognition—and the quiet, difficult work of imagining something better.
